Vice President JD Vance's role in upcoming Iran peace talks remains unclear following conflicting statements from President Trump and the White House.

Confusion Over Iran Delegation Leadership: Trump stated Vance will not lead the U.S. delegation to Iran talks in Pakistan, while the White House maintains Vance will go.

Kushner and Witkoff Tapped for Negotiations: Trump announced that Jared Kushner and Steve Witkoff will lead Iran peace talks as the administration seeks a ceasefire and shipping corridor deal.

Tucker Carlson Family Exit: Tucker Carlson's son departed from Vance's press team amid an escalating feud between the media host and President Trump.

Pope Leo XIV Backing: Vance expressed support for Pope Leo XIV as the Trump-Vance clash over Iran policy appears to be softening.

White House Damage Control: The administration is working to address what outlets are calling Trump's public snub of his vice president regarding the Iran talks assignment.

Left-leaning outlets emphasize the White House scrambling to fix apparent dysfunction and highlight fears that Vance could be recalled from Pakistan. Right-leaning sources focus more on the technical aspects of the Iran negotiations and staffing changes within Vance's team, with some framing the situation as strategic flexibility rather than internal conflict.

Vance's schedule for the Pakistan talks remains officially undetermined pending further White House clarification.
