THE VANCE DAILY - April 7, 2026

Vice President JD Vance is conducting a two-day visit to Hungary to campaign for Prime Minister Viktor Orbán ahead of upcoming elections while also backing President Trump's Iran ultimatum.

Vance campaigns for Orbán in Budapest: The Vice President is making public appearances and delivering Trump's message of support to boost Orbán's re-election bid in Hungary's upcoming elections.

Vance accuses EU of election interference: During remarks in Budapest, Vance criticized what he called "Brussels interference" in Hungarian domestic politics while defending U.S. involvement as legitimate support.

Iran ultimatum receives Vance backing: The Vice President expressed support for Trump's latest Iran ultimatum, stating "I hope they make the right response" when asked about potential military action.

Republicans questioned over Hungary trip: Some political observers are asking where Republican opposition is to Vance's high-profile campaigning for the Hungarian Prime Minister.

Vance addresses Hungarian opposition: The Vice President reportedly sent a message to opposition leader Péter Magyar regarding potential consequences if Orbán loses the election.

Left vs Right: Left-leaning outlets are emphasizing Vance's support for what they describe as Russia's favored European ally and questioning the appropriateness of U.S. election interference abroad. Right-leaning sources focus more positively on Vance's defense of Hungarian sovereignty against EU pressure and his support for Trump's Iran policy.
