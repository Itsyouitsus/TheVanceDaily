The Vance Daily - April 11, 2026

Vice President JD Vance arrived in Pakistan today to lead high-stakes diplomatic talks aimed at securing a ceasefire in the ongoing US-Israel-Iran conflict.

US-Iran Peace Talks Begin in Islamabad: Vance met with Pakistani Prime Minister Shehbaz Sharif as both American and Iranian delegations arrived in Pakistan's capital for conditional peace negotiations.

From War Critic to Diplomat: Vance, who previously opposed military action against Iran, now leads the Biden administration's efforts to end the current conflict through diplomatic channels.

Shuttle Diplomacy Strategy: The talks are being conducted through separate meetings with Pakistani leadership serving as intermediary, rather than direct US-Iran negotiations.

Hungary University Model Controversy: NBC News reported on Vance's past statements praising Viktor Orbán's policies toward universities as a model for US education reform.

High-Stakes Mission: Multiple outlets characterize these as make-or-break talks for resolving the Middle East crisis, with significant political implications for the current administration.

Left vs Right: Left-leaning outlets emphasize the irony of Vance leading peace efforts after his previous opposition to Iran military action, while also highlighting controversies like his praise of Orbán's policies. Right-leaning sources focus more positively on Vance's transition from critic to dealmaker and frame the talks as crucial diplomatic leadership.
